Biography

I always wanted to do something with art or computers. And now I am!

I have always loved games and fantasy worlds, and chose a degree in games design because I thought it looked like tremendous fun.

Well I enjoyed it so much that I decided to start a company making indie games for children, a market that just doesn't have the quality games it needs.

If you'd like to see more of my work check out my Art Station site: https://vikijohnson.artstation.com/

Experience

Game Developer

A Little Red Panda

Plymouth, UK · 2015 – Present

A Little Red Panda is my own microstudio. Within the company I aim to make beautiful interactive experiences for children to play, while also looking at using games to explore more serious topics and as a vechicle to teach in more enjoyable and innovative way.

Founder and Event Organiser

Plymouth Game Devs

Plymouth, UK · Oct 2014 – Present

I helped found a monthly meet-up group for game developers and game dev students living in Plymouth. Each month we host national speakers, tournaments, parties, as well as providing a platform for students and new companies to show off their projects and gain confidence in their work

Project Manager + 3D artist, texture creator, programmer

Plymouth Museum and Art Gallery

Plymouth, UK · Jan 2014 – Sep 2014

A collaboration between Plymouth College of Art and Plymouth City Museum and Art Gallery, I managed and led a project where we recreated a Bronze Age village, based on an internationally significant find on Dartmoor.

I worked with a group of BA students to make the village and surrounding area in 3DS Max, before building the landscape in UDK. The game was used as part of Plymouth Museum’s exhibition, from September to December 2014.
